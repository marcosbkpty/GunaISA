Culture of the Guna Wargandí People

The Guna (Dule) are Indigenous to Panama and Colombia, famed for their mola textile art—colorful, appliquéd panels crafted by women. They speak Dulegaya, preserve oral traditions via the Onmaked Nega, and socially organize through matrilineal clans.

Territory of Guna Wargandí

Comarca Kuna de Wargandí is an autonomous territory in Darien along the Sambu River. Established in 1983, it self‑governs ancestral lands and protects local ecosystems within Panama’s legal framework.
