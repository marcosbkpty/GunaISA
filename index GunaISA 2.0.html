Culture of the Guna Wargandí People

The Guna (Dule) are Indigenous to Panama and Colombia, famed for their mola textile art—colorful, appliquéd panels crafted by women. With matrilineal clans, they speak Dulegaya, preserve history via oral traditions in the Onmaked Nega, and their community sailas safeguard cultural rites and songs.

Territory of Guna Wargandí

Established in 1983, the Comarca Kuna de Wargandí is an autonomous Indigenous territory along the Sambu River in eastern Panama. It coexists with Guna Yala and Kuna de Madugandí to self‑govern ancestral lands, protect ecosystems, and uphold traditional governance under Panamanian law.
